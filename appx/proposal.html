March 26, 2026

A.1 Team Members

  • Elma – Project Coordinator & Data Curator

  • Thusa – Research Analyst & Documentation Lead

A.2 Short Project Description

This project investigates the feasibility of rental housing for college-age students (18-24 years) across U.S. states by analyzing the relationship between median rental prices and median incomes by age group. Using Zillow rental data and U.S. Census historical income tables, we will create interactive visualizations and rankings to identify which states offer the most affordable housing options for students and how housing cost burden varies by age.

A.3 Research Questions

  1. Which U.S. states are most and least affordable for college-age renters (18-24), and how does this compare to older age groups?

  2. What is the rent-to-income burden ratio for 18-24 year-olds by state, and how many states fall into “affordable” (<30%), “burdened” (30-50%), or “severely burdened” (>50%) categories?

  3. How has the affordability gap between younger renters (18-24) and established adults (35-44) changed over recent years, and which states show the most significant generational disparities in housing costs?

A.4 Reasons and Inspirations Behind Choosing the Topic

  • Personal relevance: All team members are currently students navigating housing affordability challenges
  • Policy importance: Student housing insecurity is a growing national issue affecting academic success and mental health
  • Data availability: Reliable, publicly available datasets (Zillow, Census) allow for reproducible, data-driven analysis
  • Visual impact: State-level comparisons create compelling maps and rankings that communicate findings effectively
  • Actionable insights: Results can inform prospective students, policymakers, and university administrators about regional affordability trends

A.5 Potential Data Set

Source Data Type Time Period Geographic Level Key Variables
Zillow Research Data Rental price indices 2015–present (monthly) State, Metro, County, ZIP Zillow Observed Rent Index (ZORI), median rent
U.S. Census Bureau Historical Income Tables Income by demographic characteristics Annual (2000–2023) National, State Median income by age group (18-24, 25-34, 35-44, etc.)

Data Integration: We will merge Zillow state-level median rent data with Census state-level median income data by age group to calculate rent-to-income ratios and affordability categories.

A.8 Conflict Resolution

Missed Deadline

  2. If delay affects downstream tasks, team redistributes work during next meeting
  3. If pattern emerges (2+ missed deadlines), Elma schedules 1-on-1 to discuss support needs
  4. Persistent issues escalated to instructor with team documentation

50/50 Split Decision

Elma has lead authority to initiate conflict resolution to ensure team progress 1. Each side presents argument with supporting evidence 2. Silent voting in Chat (majority wins) 3. If still deadlocked, consult instructor/preceptor for guidance 4. Document decision rationale in meeting notes
